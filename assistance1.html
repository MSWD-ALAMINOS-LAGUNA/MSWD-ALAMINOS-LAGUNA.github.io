Transportation Assistance

Assistance for the purchase or payment of transport (air/sea/land) or expenses to enable the client to return to his home province or seek further medical interventions in another place or to attend to emergency concerns such as death, critical situations of family members or other individuals in need. This includes those that require immediate presence, such as but not limited to, attendance to a court hearing, rescue of abused or trafficked relatives.


Criteria of Eligibility:


  • Must be a resident in the municipality
  • Must demonstrate an urgent need for transportation due to a crisis situation medical condition, rescue of abused, court hearing, death, and critical situations of family members.
  • Must belong to a low-income or financially distressed household


    • Specific Crisis Situations

    Covered Applicants may qualify for transportation assistance if they fall under any of the following categories:

      a. Medical-Related Emergency

  • Individuals requiring immediate medical treatment in another location due to the unavailability of necessary healthcare services in their current area.
  • Patients requiring referral or transfer to a specialized medical facility for further treatment.
  • Individuals needing medical evacuation (e.g., air or sea transport) for life-threatening conditions.


      • b. Humanitarian and Social Welfare Needs

  • Individuals or families displaced due to natural disasters, armed conflict, or other emergencies and needing transportation back to their home province or safe location.
  • Survivors of domestic abuse, human trafficking, or other forms of exploitation who need transportation for rescue, reintegration, or legal proceedings.
  • Individuals traveling to attend court hearings related to legal matters such as child custody, domestic abuse, or criminal cases involving them or their dependents.


      • c. Family and Personal Emergencies

  • Individuals needing to travel due to the death of an immediate family member (e.g., spouse, parent, child, or sibling).
  • Individuals who must urgently attend to a critical health situation of a close family member in another location.
  • Individuals needing to assist in the rescue or recovery of a missing, kidnapped, or trafficked relative.

      • Requirements


    1. Updated Original Certificate of Indigency from Barangay with proof of low income
    2. Medical Certificate referral (for medical transport request)
    3. Death Certificate or Medical Report of a Relative (for family emergency)
    4. Police Report or legal documents (for cases of abuse, transferring or court related cases).
    5. ID of authorized representative and Authorization Letter
    6. ID of patient
    7. Proof of Relationship (Birth Certificate, Marriage Certificate)
